What

Catigbian, officially the Municipality of Catigbian (Cebuano: Munisipyo sa Catigbian; Tagalog: Bayan ng Catigbian), is a 4th class municipality in the province of Bohol, Philippines. Based on the latest available census data as of 2026, Catigbian continues to grow as one of Bohol’s developing inland municipalities, known for its agricultural industry, livestock trading, tourism spots, and peaceful rural communities.

Formerly known as San Jacinto, the municipality officially adopted the name “Catigbian” in 1954. The name originated from the “Katigbi” plant (Coix lacryma-jobi), a seed-bearing plant that once grew abundantly in the area.

Catigbian was founded in 1829 as one of the settlements where rebels were relocated following the suppression of the historic Dagohoy Rebellion. In 1903, during the reorganization of the province of Bohol, the town was annexed to Balilihan before regaining its status as an independent municipality on June 17, 1949.

Located approximately 34 kilometers north of Tagbilaran City, Catigbian is bordered by the municipalities of Balilihan, Sagbayan, Tubigon, San Isidro, and Batuan. The municipality is politically subdivided into 22 barangays, each consisting of puroks and several sitios.

Today, Catigbian is recognized for its growing local economy, educational institutions, eco-tourism destinations, and community-centered development programs. The municipality also continues to celebrate its culture and heritage through local festivals, public events, and agricultural activities that reflect the identity and traditions of the Catigbianons.
